This was a group project for the Efficient Algorithms and Intractable Problems course at UC Berkeley.

It approximates a solution to the following problem: Given a group of children, friendship relations, n buses and a set of trouble groups, find an assignment of students to the buses that maximizes the number of friendship relations that are on the same bus. The difficulty comes from this added condition: Groups of children that form a trouble group and are all assigned to the same bus do not have their friendships counted.

My group's solver used a combination of efficient greedy algorithms with various heuristics as well as some local search algorithms. Note that the details of our solver can be found in a write-up that is included in the project repo.

Achievements: My groupâ€™s solution was in the top 10% of all approximations in the course (the ranking metric is explained in the repo). I also learned to coordinate and Kutilize the expertise of my team-mates as we all had different backgrounds (EE, Math, CS).
